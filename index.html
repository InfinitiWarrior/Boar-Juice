About the Estate

A Beverage of
Terrifying Distinction

Boar Juice is the natural conclusion of spending too long in Revelwood. The estate operates without permits, without a governing body, and without a cellarmaster who will commit to a calendar. What it lacks in regulation it compensates for in character.

Each batch is hand-harvested from Fell Boar territories where the Shroud runs deepest and the respawn timer runs longest. The process is proprietary. The results are not guaranteed.

Sommelier's Notes

Tasting Profile

01 — Nose

The Approach

Damp forest floor, singed fur, and ancient shroud mist. Complex secondary notes of what can only be described as determined malevolence.

02 — Palate

The Entry

Bold and uncompromising. Thick with tannins of desperation. The mid-palate reveals unexpected courage, shortly before respawning.

03 — Finish

The Aftermath

Long, haunting, and slightly aggressive. Lingers on the soul well past death. Pairs with iron pickaxes and a long walk back to your corpse.

04 — Composition

The Substance

Free-range boar essence, fell ichor, corrupted tusk residue, and the quiet regret of someone who has done this before and learned nothing.

From Fell Beast to Fine Beverage

The Art of
Production

An ancient craft, refined through countless deaths and moderate amounts of fieldwork. Documented here for posterity and mild entertainment.

Credit: GIANTENEMYBIRD

01

The Encounter

The production begins, as all great things do, with poor planning and overconfidence. The prospective producer enters the Shroud equipped with a knife, a dream, and insufficient armour. Brief eye contact with the boar is established. The boar does not share your vision.

02

The Squeeze

Extraction is achieved through the application of considerable downward force. The process is proprietary. The boar is not consulted. What emerges is something the industry has not yet found adequate language for.

03

The Juice

The result. Aged briefly or not at all, depending on patience levels and proximity to a suitable vessel. Served at cellar temperature. The estate takes no responsibility for what happens next.
